Sinigang na Baboy (Pork in Tamarind Soup)

Description

Sinigang is a Filipino soup with a sour flavor. It comes from the Tagalog word "sigang", which means "to stew". It primarily uses sampaloc (tamarind) as the souring ingredient, but it can replaced with other native ingredients such as calamansi, santol, unripe indian mangoes, etc.

In this recipe, we will be using a ready-to-use tamarind mix from Knorr instead of using raw tamarind for convenience.

Ingredients

  • ½ kg pork spareribs
  • ½ kg pork belly
  • 2 tomatoes
  • 2 red onions
  • 4 siling haba
  • 1 bundle of kangkong (water spinach)
  • 2 eggplants
  • 5 okras
  • 1 large pack of Knorr Sinigang sa Sampaloc Mix (Original)

Steps

  1. Clean the meat and vegetables with running water.
  2. Chop off the hard stem of the water spinach.
  3. Slice each tomato into 6 pieces.
  4. Slice each eggplant into 6 pieces and remove the stem.
  5. Slice each okra in half.
  6. Dice the red onions.
  7. Slice pork spareribs into small pieces.
  8. Prepare and heat up a teaspoon of cooking oil in a pot at medium heat.
  9. Sautee the tomatoes and onions in the pot until the onions turn translucent.
  10. Add the pork ingredients to the pot and cook until brown.
  11. Add water to the pot until the pork meat are submerged.
  12. Change to high heat, cover the pot, then bring the water to a boil.
  13. While boiling, uncover the pot, add the sinigang mix then change to medium heat.
  14. Cover the pot again, and check every now and then to see if pork is tender.
  15. Once the pork is tender, uncover the pot and add the okras and eggplants.
  16. Cover the pot and wait 10 minutes.
  17. Turn off the fire, uncover the pot, then add the water spinach.
  18. Cover the pot, the let water spinach cook on its own for around 5 minutes.
